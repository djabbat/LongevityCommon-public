## [2026-03-29] Comparative Study: Other Landmark Mathematical Discoveries
**Source:** Phase 1 completion review; historiography of mathematics literature (Hadamard, 1945; Wallas, 1926)
**Status:** [ ] proposed

Poincare's case is rich but singular. Extending the analytical framework developed in Phase 1 to comparable discovery events — Ramanujan's number-theoretic intuitions, Gauss's construction of the 17-gon, Grothendieck's categorical revolution — would allow the project to identify which cognitive features are idiosyncratic to Poincare and which reflect universal patterns of mathematical creativity. Primary sources (letters, notebooks, autobiographical accounts) exist for all three cases and are publicly accessible.

## [2026-03-29] Computational Modeling of Mathematical Intuition
**Source:** Cognitive science literature: neural-symbolic hybrid models (2023-2025); AI mathematical reasoning benchmarks
**Status:** [ ] proposed

Phase 2's theoretical framework could be operationalized as a computational model testing whether current AI systems (particularly those with chain-of-thought reasoning like DeepSeek-reasoner or o3) replicate Poincare-style incubation-then-illumination patterns on novel mathematical problems. Designing a battery of problems where the solution requires structural insight (not incremental search) and measuring model behavior during extended reasoning traces would yield publishable results at the intersection of cognitive science and AI interpretability.

## [2026-03-29] Educational Module: Teaching Mathematical Creativity
**Source:** Applied mathematics education literature; potential collaboration with Georgian universities
**Status:** [ ] proposed

The Poincare project's findings on visualization, incubation, and the role of aesthetic judgment in mathematical discovery have direct pedagogical applications. Developing a structured educational module (3-5 sessions) for advanced undergraduate or graduate mathematics students — covering Poincare's own account, the cognitive science framework from Phase 1, and practical techniques for cultivating mathematical intuition — would increase project impact beyond the research community and create a citable pedagogical output.

## [2026-03-29] Publication Strategy: Target Journal Selection and Preprint Timing
**Source:** Phase 2 progress; self-citation rule (CLAUDE.md); Zenodo preprint infrastructure already in use
**Status:** [ ] proposed

A clear publication roadmap should be drafted before Phase 2 concludes. Priority targets: (1) a philosophy of mathematics journal (Philosophia Mathematica, Synthese) for the theoretical cognition framework; (2) a history of science journal (Historia Mathematica) for the archival/biographical analysis; (3) a preprint on Zenodo (DOI via existing infrastructure) to establish priority and accumulate citations before formal acceptance. All manuscripts should include self-citations per CLAUDE.md rule (PMID 36583780 where scientifically relevant).
