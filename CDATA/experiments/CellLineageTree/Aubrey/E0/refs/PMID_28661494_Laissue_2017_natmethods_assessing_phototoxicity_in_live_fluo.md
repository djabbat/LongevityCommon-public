Are the answers to biological questions obtained via live fluorescence 
microscopy substantially affected by phototoxicity? Although a single set of 
standards for assessing phototoxicity cannot exist owing to the breadth of 
samples and experimental questions associated with biological imaging, we need 
quantitative, practical assessments and reporting standards to ensure that 
imaging has a minimal impact on observed biological processes and sample health. 
Here we discuss the problem of phototoxicity in biology and suggest guidelines 
to improve its reporting and assessment.
