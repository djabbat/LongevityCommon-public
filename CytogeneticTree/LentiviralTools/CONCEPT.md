# LentiviralTools — Plasmid Design, Packaging, and Clonal Line Generation

**Parent project:** [CytogeneticTree](../CONCEPT.md)

## §1 Purpose

The Cytogenetic Tree project depends on **stable, clonal cell lines** expressing the RITE-centriole cassette (and future variants: triple-tag, organelle reporters, differentiation markers). This subproject is the molecular workshop: plasmid design (Twist / Addgene), HEK293T-based lentivirus packaging, BJ-hTERT transduction, and single-cell clonal isolation with karyotyping.

## §2 Scientific basis / mechanism

Third-generation lentiviral system (pMD2.G VSV-G, psPAX2 packaging, self-inactivating LTRs) enables integration of payloads up to ~ 8 kb into dividing + non-dividing mammalian cells at 10⁶–10⁸ TU/mL titers. Clonal lines are generated by single-cell FACS into 96-well plates, expanded, karyotyped (G-banding or SKY), and QC'd for marker expression. Quality control must include: (a) confirmed integration site(s) via inverse PCR or TAIL-PCR, (b) clone uniformity across ≥ 3 passages, (c) preserved karyotype, (d) functional RITE response to tamoxifen.

## §5 Known gaps + what this subproject builds

**Gaps:**
1. Large RITE cassettes (~ 5 kb) approach lentiviral payload limit
2. Clonal uniformity often poor without single-integration selection
3. Long-term silencing of integrated reporter is a known failure mode (needs UCOE / insulator elements)

**Deliverables (Phase A):**
- Design + synthesize 3 RITE-centriole plasmids (in coordination with `RITE_Centriole`)
- Establish BSL-2 lentivirus packaging workflow in host lab
- Produce high-titer virus (≥ 10⁷ TU/mL) for each construct
- Generate ≥ 2 clonal BJ-hTERT-RITE lines per construct, karyotyped + QC'd
- SOPs deposited on Zenodo
